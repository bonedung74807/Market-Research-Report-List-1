<p><p>Linear accelerators (Linacs) for radiation therapy are categorized into low-energy and high-energy types based on their energy output. Low-energy Linacs, typically used for treating superficial tumors, deliver lower radiation doses, making them ideal for targeting skin cancers and lesions. In contrast, high-energy Linacs are designed to penetrate deeper tissues, effectively treating more invasive tumors like those found in the lung or prostate. Both types are essential for precise radiation therapy, enhancing treatment outcomes and patient care.</p></p>
