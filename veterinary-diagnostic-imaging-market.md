<p><p>The Veterinary Diagnostic Imaging Market encompasses various imaging modalities, each serving distinct diagnostic purposes. X-ray imaging is commonly used for assessing bones and detecting foreign objects. Ultrasound offers real-time visualization of soft tissues and internal organs, making it ideal for monitoring pregnancies and examining the abdomen. MRI provides detailed images of soft tissues, enhancing the diagnosis of neurological conditions and complex musculoskeletal issues. Other imaging techniques may include computed tomography (CT) and nuclear imaging, further expanding diagnostic capabilities in veterinary medicine.</p></p>
